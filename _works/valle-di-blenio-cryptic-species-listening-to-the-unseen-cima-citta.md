  EN:

  In biology, “cryptic species” are species that cannot be distinguished
  morphologically, that is by looking at their shape, structure and form, and
  yet belong to a different species. Recognizing their difference requires a
  process that extends beyond the visible surface. Translated outside of its
  biological context, “cryptic species” opens a variety of interpretive
  possibilities. Cryptic species elude legibility, communicate in code, appear
  ambiguous and enigmatic. They are difficult or impossible to interpret or
  grasp. The engagement with cryptic species therefore demands processes and
  approaches that move outside established structures, norms and laws.

EN:

In biology, “cryptic species” are species that cannot be distinguished morphologically, that is by looking at their shape, structure and form, and yet belong to a different species. Recognizing their difference requires a process that extends beyond the visible surface. Translated outside of its biological context, “cryptic species” opens a variety of interpretive possibilities. Cryptic species elude legibility, communicate in code, appear ambiguous and enigmatic. They are difficult or impossible to interpret or grasp. The engagement with cryptic species therefore demands processes and approaches that move outside established structures, norms and laws.
