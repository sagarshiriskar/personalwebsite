**Editorial documentary photo series.**

This project follows Tukaram, a third-generation dhobi at the Film and Television Institute of India, whose father worked at the legendary Prabhat Film Company.

When floods destroyed his family’s photographs in 1961, Tukaram lost the only visual proof of his father’s connection to a golden era of Indian cinema, an era that included figures such as V. Shantaram, Dev Anand, and Guru Dutt.

The series documents a man who bridges past and present; once delivering laundry to film icons, now serving a new generation of filmmakers on the same historic grounds.
